## Summary

This applied study examined the use of tactile presentation during picture-book storytelling to evoke children’s interest. The study focused on shared haptics as an interaction design approach for enriching storytelling experiences.

The study contributes to the Supporting and HCI lines by exploring how tactile feedback can be embedded in child-facing interaction design. It positions haptic presentation as a way to shape engagement during human–human interaction around storytelling.

## Research line

- **Supporting**: technology-mediated support for interaction and engagement
- **HCI**: shared haptics and interaction design for children
- **Storytelling**: tactile augmentation of picture-book reading contexts
