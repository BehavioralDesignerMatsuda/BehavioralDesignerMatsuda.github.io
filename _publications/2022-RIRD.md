## Summary

This study examined the effects of an intervention combining Response Interruption and Redirection (RIRD) with script presentation on reducing solitary speech behavior in a child with autism spectrum disorder. The study also evaluated whether the procedure could be applied by a caregiver.

The study contributes to the Supporting and Autism Support lines by examining how a behavior-analytic intervention can be arranged in a form that is applicable in everyday family contexts. It also highlights parent-mediated implementation as an important condition for translating intervention procedures into practical support.

## Research line

* **Supporting**: behavior-analytic support for children and families
* **Autism Support**: intervention for solitary speech behavior in a child with autism spectrum disorder
* **Parent-Mediated Support**: evaluation of caregiver applicability in everyday contexts
