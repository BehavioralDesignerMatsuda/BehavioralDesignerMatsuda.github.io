## Summary

This systematic review examined how speech has been measured in studies of children and individuals with selective mutism. The review focused on assessment methods, including diagnostic confirmation and differential assessment procedures, and organized how speech-related outcomes have been evaluated across prior studies.

The study contributes to the Measuring line by clarifying the observation and assessment units used in selective mutism research. It also provides a basis for designing future direct observation and measurement procedures for speech in different social contexts.

## Research line

- **Measuring**: review of speech assessment methods and observation units
- **Selective Mutism**: assessment of speech across social contexts
- **Systematic Review**: mapping prior measurement practices and diagnostic confirmation methods
